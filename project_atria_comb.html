01 · The Challenge

One desk.
Five problems.
One form.

The modern desk accumulates objects without a coherent storage logic — pens, cards, cables, keys, small accessories, and medication occupy separate containers or simply pile up. No single product addresses this multiplicity within a compact, elegant footprint.

The brief: design and manufacture a desktop organiser in a single 3D print session, within a strict 30-metre filament limit, that integrates five distinct storage functions through geometry alone — no assembly, no fasteners.

02 · The Design · Prototype

Five functions,
physically
verified.

The PLA prototype was printed on a Bambu Lab X1 Carbon as a single continuous form. All five integrated systems were demonstrated in real use — confirming the tolerance design, snap-fit mechanics, and guide-rail assembly logic.

I

3× Pen Holders — stores stationery inside, holds pens on the outer cylinder surface

II

Card Holder — two deep back-panel slots use PLA elasticity to snap-clamp 2× standard 85×55×0.8mm cards

III

Cable Storage Box — rotating lid with ±0.3mm slide rail; screw in cable, pull both ends to release

IV

Hanging Rack — back-panel truss arm holds keys, jewellery, sticky notes

V

Pill Drawer — guide-rail drawer assembled via precision slide rail; stores pills or small accessories

04 · The Engineering · DfAM Implementation

Design for
additive manufacturing.

Every geometric decision was made with FDM constraints and capabilities in mind — from truss topology to print orientation, from infill density to tolerance zoning. The goal: maximum function within a minimum material envelope.

Integrated geometry

3D printing allows the wire storage box, pill drawer, and pen holder body to be combined into a single continuous form — no assembly joints, no fasteners between primary volumes.

Two deep slots in the back panel exploit PLA's inherent elasticity for snap-fit card retention. The Bambu Lab X1 Carbon's high-precision nozzle directly engraves logos into the 1mm wall surface.

Truss topology · material constraint

Solid volume is replaced with a lightweight truss system in all non-load-bearing regions — balancing load distribution while keeping total filament at 29.68m, within the strict 30m limit. The truss also defines the product's visual language.

Print process optimisation

01

One G-Code: all components fit on a single plate in one uninterrupted print run.

02

Build time 3h 40min: honeycomb sparse infill at 10% density optimises walls without sacrificing structural integrity.

03

Print orientation: printing from the base (not the back panel) prevents 'spaghetti' failure on the 1mm thin wall.
